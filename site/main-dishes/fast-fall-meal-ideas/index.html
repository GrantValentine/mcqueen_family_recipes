    Fast Fall Meal Ideas

    Main dishes
    🍽

    Ingredients

    • Some of my “go-to” meals when kids were all young.
    • Fast Side Dishes:
    • Couscous – Cook in broth or water with seasonings if desired
    • Israeli or Pearl Couscous
    • Fast cooking quinoa
    • Ratatouille from Harmon’s deli
    • Alexia’s sweet potato fries – sprinkle with Cavendar’s Greek seasoning, bake as directed.
    • 1. Chopped Cauliflower or Broccoli
    • Drizzle juice from 1 orange on top of cauliflower or broccoli
    • Drizzle EVOO on top, mix with hands, sprinkle with kosher salt and pepper. Bake for 15-18 min.
    • 2. Root vegies – Chop choice of fingerling potatoes, red potatoes, sweet potatoes, turnips, celery root, celery, kohlrabi bulbs, beets, onions, whole garlic cloves – Drizzle EVOO on top, mix around with hands, sprinkle with kosher salt and pepper. Bake for 40 minutes.
    • 3. Butternut squash or zucchini or other squash – Chop in pieces (or buy already chopped!), place in pan, Drizzle EVOO on top, press 2-3 cloves of garlic on top, mix with hands, bake for 30 minutes.
    • Fast Sandwiches:
    • French Dip:
    • Thinly sliced roast beef (recommend Boar’s Head, Target brand is also pretty good)
    • French rolls, sliced
    • Butter
    • Provolone cheese
    • 2 cans Campbell French Onion soup
    • water
    • 2. Ciabatta, chicken, marinara sauce, mozzarella or provolone cheese
    • 3. Firm sandwich bread, turkey, artichoke pesto, tomatoes, Havarti cheese
    • 4. Caprese – fresh mozzarella, basil leaves, tomato slices, drizzle of balsamic vinegar
    • 5. Proscuitto, fontina or Coastal cheese, spinach leaves
    • 6. Pizza panini – tomato or pizza sauce, fresh mozzarella, basil slices
    • Eggs:
    • French Omelet Bar
    • Set up filling choices – bacon, ham, chopped tomatoes, chopped green onions, spinach, grated cheeses, bay shrimp, sliced avocados, chopped vegetables
    • Whip 3 eggs well with 3 TB water. Butter omelet pan, pour ½ of egg mixture in each side. Cook over low-med heat until almost set. Fill with your choice of filling. Flip pan. Cook over low heat for 2-3 minutes.
    • Toad-in-the-hole
    • 12 slices white bread
    • ½ lb. sliced sharp Cheddar cheese (or other cheese of choice)
    • 4 eggs, beaten
    • 2 ½ c. whole milk
    • ½ tsp. prepared mustard
    • ½ tsp. salt and pepper

    Instructions

    1. 1 Roasted Vegetables – My favorite fast way to cook vegies. Line a roasting pan with aluminum foil. Preheat oven to 425 degrees (use convection setting if you have convection oven.) Roasting brings out the flavor.
    2. 2 Try these combinations:
    3. 3 Mix 2 cans of soup and 1 can of water in saucepan, heat.
    4. 4 Slice French rolls, butter and toast under broiler. Layer roast beef on bottom side of roll, place Provolone cheese on top. Return to broiler to melt cheese. Put top on sandwich. Serve with soup dip. (If you want to be fancier, slice 2 onions and caramelize in frying pan with butter – cook on low-med heat until golden and yummy or top with carmelized onions from the can.)
    5. 5 Paninis: Try these combinations – 1. Ciabatta, basil pesto, goat cheese, red pepper strips
    6. 6 Cut out circle from piece of white bread, melt butter in frying pan or griddle, put bread in pan, crack egg and put in center hole of bread, place circle in pan and toast on both sides. Once lightly set, flip bread over and cook on other side. Serve with toasted middle.
    7. 7 Cheese Strata
    8. 8 Arrange 6 slices of bread in a buttered 13 x 9 inch pan. Top with cheese slices, then top with additional bread slices. Place all other ingredients in blender and mix well. Pour egg mixture over bread. Cover and place in refrigerator for several hours. Bake at 325 for 40 minutes.

    Notes & Tips

    • Bacon Pie
    • Pillsbury refrigerated pie crust
    • 2 ½ cup – 3 C grated Swiss cheese
    • 6-8 slices of bacon, cooked and chopped
    • 5 eggs
    • 1 cup half-n-half
    • 1 ½ cups milk
    • ½ tsp Salt and ¼ tsp. pepper
    • Preheat oven to 425 degrees. Unroll pie crust, crimp edge. Sprinkle bacon in bottom of crust, then top with Swiss cheese. Place eggs, milk, half-n-half, salt and pepper in blender, mix well. Pour egg mixture on top of bacon/cheese. Bake at 425 degrees for 15 minutes, reduce oven temp to 350 degrees, and bake for additional 30 minutes. Remove from oven, let rest 5 minutes, cut and serve.
    • Favorite leftover meat idea:
    • Puff pastry shells – bake as directed
    • Use with left-over roast or chicken. Mix 2 cans Cream of Mushroom soup and 1 can whole milk in pan. Whisk and heat, stir in left over meat.
    • When puff pastry shells done, remove top and middle portion. Ladle in soup/meat mixture, place top on. Serve.
    • Crock-Pot
    • Cream Cheese Chicken
    • Place 1/3 – ½ package frozen chicken tenderloins (from Costco) in bottom of crock pot. Add 1 can of cream of chicken soup, 1 8-oz. block of cream cheese (cubed), and 1 package Zesty Italian dry recipe package. Cook on low for 8 hours. Stir every 2-3 hours. Serve with rice.
    • Meatballs
    • Place ½ package of frozen meatballs (from Costco) in bottom or crock pot. Add 2 bottles of Heinz chili sauce, 32 oz. grape jelly, 3-4 crushed garlic cloves, dashes of Tabasco. Cook on low for about 8 hours. Serve on rolls, over rice, or as an appetizer.
    • Mexican chicken
    • Place 1/3-1/2 package of frozen chicken tenderloins, sliced onions, sliced bell peppers. Top with 1 can Rotel original tomatoes and 1 can mild enchilada sauce. Cook on low for about 8 hours. Serve with tortillas.
