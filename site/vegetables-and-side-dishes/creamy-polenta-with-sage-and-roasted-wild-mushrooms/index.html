    Creamy Polenta with Sage and Roasted Wild Mushrooms

    Vegetables and Side Dishes
    🍽

    Ingredients

    • Polenta
    • 1¾ cups water
    • 1¾ cups chicken broth
    • 1 teaspoon minced garlic
    • ¾ cup polenta (or cornmeal)
    • ⅔ cup crème fraîche or sour cream
    • 1 ounce Monterey Jack cheese, shredded (about ¼ cup)
    • 1 ounce Parmesan cheese, freshly grated (about ¼ cup)
    • 3 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted
    • ¼ teaspoon salt
    • ⅛ teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
    • Crispy Sage
    • ½ cup extra-virgin olive oil
    • ⅓ cup fresh sage leaves
    • Salt
    • Roasted Wild Mushrooms
    • 8 cloves garlic, thinly sliced
    • 3 tablespoons olive oil
    • 3 tablespoons balsamic vinegar or red wine vinegar
    • 3 sprigs fresh rosemary, chopped
    • 3 sprigs fresh thyme, chopped
    • 1 pound large fresh wild mushrooms (shiitake, oyster, or cremini)
    • ¼ teaspoon salt
    • ⅛ teaspoon freshly ground black pepper

    Instructions

    1. 1 Preheat oven to 350°F.
    2. 2 Bring water, chicken broth, and garlic to a boil in a large ovenproof saucepan. Slowly whisk in polenta.
    3. 3 Reduce heat to medium and cook, stirring constantly, for about 5 minutes. Cover and place pan in oven.
    4. 4 Bake until thick but still creamy, stirring occasionally, about 45 minutes. Add additional water if mixture seems dry.
    5. 5 Heat olive oil in a small skillet over medium-high heat. Fry sage leaves until crisp, about 10 seconds. Drain on paper towels and season lightly with salt. Set aside.
    6. 6 Stir crème fraîche, Monterey Jack, Parmesan, butter, salt, and pepper into the polenta until smooth.
    7. 7 Spoon polenta onto serving plates, top with roasted mushrooms, garnish with sage, and serve immediately.
    8. 8 Roasted Wild Mushrooms
    9. 9 Preheat oven to 425°F. Line two baking sheets with foil.
    10. 10 Toss garlic, olive oil, vinegar, rosemary, thyme, and mushrooms in a large bowl. Season with salt and pepper.
    11. 11 Arrange mushrooms in a single layer and roast until tender and slightly crisp at edges, about 25 minutes. Serve immediately.
